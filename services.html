Our Services

Radiology/Medical Imaging

Medical radiography is a broad term that refers to a variety of studies that require the use of x-ray techniques to visualize internal organs.

Medical Laboratory Services

Laboratory medicine is an essential component of public health. It is critical for disease detection in individuals and populations. Laboratory tests are also useful for detecting environmental toxins like lead.

Molecular Science Genetic Studies

Molecular genetics (MG) is a scientific discipline concerned with the structure and function of genes at the molecular level. It also includes genetic engineering, which is defined as the direct manipulation of an organism’s genome.

Medical-Laboratory Services

01. Medical Microbiology & Parasitology

Our Clinical microbiology tests isolate and characterize infectious organisms (e.g. bacteria, fungi, viruses, and parasites) so that the diseases they cause can be managed and treated in patients.

02.Clinical Biochemistry

Clinical chemistry tests are used to determine the concentrations of chemical components in bodily fluids and tissues. The results of these tests are then used to evaluate the functions of body organs. Chemical components such as blood glucose, proteins, electrolytes, lipids, and other metabolic substances are detected and measured using clinical chemistry tests.

03. Immunology

The Immunology Laboratory is in charge of laboratory testing and clinical consultation in a variety of areas, including autoimmune disease, immunodeficiencies, immunoproliferative disorders, and allergy, as well as some aspects of infectious disease serology.

04. Haematology

We perform credible routine hematology testing and various body fluid testing and analysis for clinical assessments of blood cells and blood disorders.

05. Histopathology & Cytology

We perform credible routine histopathology and cytology testing and various body fluid testing and analysis for clinical assessments of tissues.

06. Molecular Science & Genetic Studies

We study the genetic makeup of people to understand how the molecules that make up cells determine the behavior of living things.

Radiography/ Medical Imaging

01. Digital X-Ray

Digital radiography (DR) is an advanced form of x-ray inspection that generates a digital radiographic image on a computer instantly

02. Ultrasonography

Diagnostic ultrasound, also known as sonography or diagnostic medical sonography, is a type of imaging that uses sound waves to create images of structures within your body. The images can provide useful information for diagnosing and treating a wide range of diseases and conditions.

03. Digital Mammography

Digital mammography, also known as full-field digital mammography (FFDM), is a type of mammography that replaces X-ray film with solid-state detectors that convert X-rays into electrical signals. These detectors resemble those used in digital cameras.

04. Cardiotocography

Cardiotocography (CTG) is a method of continuously recording the fetal heart rate using an ultrasound transducer placed on the mother’s abdomen. CTG is widely used in pregnancy to assess fetal wellbeing, particularly in pregnancies with a high risk of complications.

05. Echocardiography

An echocardiogram is an ultrasound examination of your heart’s structure and function. An echo can detect a variety of conditions such as cardiomyopathy and valve disease. Transthoracic and transesophageal echo tests are two types of echo tests.

06. 12 Channel Electrocardiography

The electrical activity of the heart is graphically represented by an electrocardiograph (or ECG system). Electrodes placed on the skin’s surface collect this activity. Electrocardiography analysis can detect heart problems such as recent or ongoing heart attacks, arrhythmia, coronary artery blockage, damaged heart muscle, dilated heart volume, and so on.

07. Electroencephalography

An electroencephalogram (EEG) is a test that uses small metal discs (electrodes) attached to the scalp to measure electrical activity in the brain. Brain cells communicate via electrical impulses and are constantly active, even while sleeping.

08. Computed Tomography

CT scanning is another name for computed tomography. A CT scan is a diagnostic imaging procedure that produces images of the inside of the body using X-rays and computer technology. It displays detailed images of any part of the body, such as bones, muscles, fat, organs, and blood vessels.

09. Magnetic Resonance Imaging

Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is a medical imaging technique that creates detailed images of your body’s organs and tissues by using a magnetic field and computer-generated radio waves.

Molecular Science Genetic Studies

01. DNA Sequencing and Analysis

Sequencing DNA entails determining the order of the four chemical building blocks that comprise the DNA molecule, known as “bases.” The sequence informs scientists about the type of genetic information carried by a specific DNA segment.

02. Microarray and Genotyping

Microarrays are an excellent platform for evaluating known markers in the human genome, allowing researchers to identify single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) or larger structural changes among millions of markers

03. Cytogenetics

Cytogenetics is important in detecting chromosomal abnormalities associated with cancer, as well as identifying new alterations that allow for more research and knowledge about the genetic aspects of these diseases.

04. Fluoresence in situ hybridization

A laboratory technique for examining genes or chromosomes in cells and tissues.

05. Specialized and BLS-3 Facility

BSL-3 laboratories are used to research infectious agents or toxins that can spread through the air and cause potentially fatal infections. All experiments are carried out in a biosafety cabinet. BSL-3 laboratories are intended to be simple to decontaminate.

06. Paternity Testing

A DNA paternity test determines whether a man is the child’s biological father by extracting DNA from a cheek swab.

07. Genetic Analysis

The examination of a DNA sample for mutations (changes) that may increase the risk of disease or affect how a person responds to treatment.

08. Covid-19 test

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is a viral infection caused by a recently discovered coronavirus. Following a covid outbreak, it is ideal to take the test for prompt case identification, prudent treatment of positive cases, and immediate isolation to prevent spread. Early testing also aids in identifying anyone who has come into contact with infected people, allowing them to be treated as soon as possible. We provide accurate, timely, and dependable testing services to facilitate proper management of positive cases and to assist negative clients in traveling safely.
